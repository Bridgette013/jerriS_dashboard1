Vol 1. The Mental Game

01. Car-Cry Sanctuary

The driveway is your decompression chamber. Let it happen. Then go inside and be the mom they need. Keep tissues and water in the console.

02. No Comparison

Stop comparing your Day 1 to an influencer's Day 1000. They have help you don't see. Your only competition is yesterday.

03. Ask for help

You are not weak for needing help. Text the friend. Call the family. People want to help—let them in before you're drowning.

04. Therapy = Maintenance

You change the oil in your car so the engine doesn't blow. Therapy is the same concept. BetterHelp and Open Path Collective offer affordable options.

05. Present > Perfect

Burnt dinner or screen time doesn't make you a bad mom. Showing up every day does. That is more than enough.

Vol 2. Money & Survival

06. Apply for Everything

WIC, SNAP, TANF, Utility Assistance. These programs exist for this moment. Forget the middleman—go straight to the source.

07. The $25 Starter

Open a savings account with $25 and never touch it. Auto-transfer $5-10/week. Emergency funds start small, but they start today.

08. Track Every Penny

One month. Every coffee, every drive-thru. No judgment—just awareness. You can't fix what you can't see.

09. File for Support

Even if he won't pay, the court order creates a legal record. It protects your kids long-term. Let the system handle the fight.

10. One Marketable Skill

Bookkeeping, social media, coding, notary. Learn one skill that pays more than minimum wage. YouTube University is free.

Vol 3. The Mini-Team

11. They are Watching

You don't have to hide the struggle. Let them see you problem-solve, recover, and keep going. That's the real lesson.

12. Routine = Sanity

Backpacks on hooks. Shoes by the door. Small systems prevent morning meltdowns. Start with one today.

13. Delegation Duty

Let them help. Age-appropriate chores aren't unfair; they're family contributions. They are learning life skills.

14. 10 Minute Dates

Undivided attention for 10 minutes beats an expensive outing. Walk to the mailbox. Read an extra book. Just be there.

15. Drop the Dad-Guilt

You can't control another adult. Focus on what happens under your roof. Stability and love are enough.

Vol 4. Base Camp Ops

16. Healthy vs Clean

If the health department wouldn't shut you down, you're fine. Toys mean kids live here. Lower the bar.

17. 10-Min Nightly Reset

Timer for 10 min. Clear counters. Start dishwasher. Prep coffee. Your morning sanity is built tonight.

18. Paper Plate Victory

If paper plates mean 20 more minutes of sanity, use them. Nobody is inspecting your dishware.

19. Reality Meal Prep

Cook double on Sunday. Leftovers on Tuesday. Frozen nuggets are a win. Fed is fed.

20. Automate Bills

Auto-pay everything. One less thing to remember when you're running on fumes.

Vol 5. The CEO (You)

21. Hydrate or Die

Actual water. Not Diet Coke. Not coffee. Your body is running a marathon on fumes—fuel it.

22. 15 Min Movement

Not to lose weight—to feel human. Walk, dance, or find a quick YouTube workout. Move the blood.

23. Protect Your Identity

A podcast, a journal, a bath. You existed before them and you'll exist after. Keep a hobby that's yours.

24. Find Your Tribe

Single moms who get it. Search Facebook for local groups. You need a safe space to vent with people who know the struggle.

25. You are Doing It

You got up. You fed them. You finished this list. That's everything. You are doing better than you think.
